Consulting

Venture Strategy Solutions

Semester 1: Driver, Inc./Internal Project

As a freshman at UC Berkeley, I was thrilled to hear about the opportunities presented to me by the hundreds of organizations around campus. After hearing about consulting, I was immediately captured by the work done by consulting clubs on campus and to align that with my interest in entrepreneurship, I decided to apply to Venture Strategy Solutions.

After a rigorous application process, I was fortunate to be among 7 new members of an initial 200 applicant pool. I immediately dove into the work by joining the team consulting for Driver, Inc. Driver was a start-up that aggregated all clinical trials and cancer treatments to recommend the best possible process for cancer patients based on data analysis.

Driver wanted us to spend the first half being external consultants: learning about the market of healthcare technology and constructing a Chinese penetration strategy for their recent product launch. My primary task was to analyze the competitors of Driver and note what made those companies succeed (especially in China).

Unfortunately due to the volatile start-up culture, Driver went bankrupt half way through the semester, leaving my team without a project. We decided to embark upon a Venture internal project to improve its branding and recruitment process. Venture has been maintaining the same website for the past few decades and unfortunately, it is out-of-date and leading to many unwanted biases within the new member pool.

In addition, I am working with a friend/co-consultant to completely redesign and reinvent Venture's website to attract more clients as well as applicants for the organization.

Dance

“I’m not sure I can do this,” I thought, panicked by the idea of dancing with more than 1,000 of my fellow students in the audience. I had never performed in front of anyone prior to this moment, much less choreographed anything. Was I was about to make a fool of myself?

My heart pounded, and my leg muscles shook as I forced myself onstage. Then, the familiar music began, and I surrendered myself to the rhythms. My fears drained away as I focused on remembering the moves I had designed for myself. At first I felt clumsy following the beat, but the audience helped me relax by clapping in time. I was thrilled to find myself entertaining people and smiled broadly as my hands interpreted the lyrics and my feet snapped to position.

My enthusiasm grew with the positive response, and when the two minutes and thirty seconds had passed and I ran off the stage to a roar of approval, I thought, “I wish the song could have gone on longer.” Little did I know that this freshman dance, part of my new school’s homecoming celebration, was the birth of an obsession. I wanted to dance for the rest of my life.

In fact, dance has become my life; I currently dance on a competitive Bollywood, Hip-Hop, and Bhangra team at UC Berkeley called Zahanat and continue to dance at sports rallies and entertain at parties. I had continued choreographing for Homecoming as a sophomore, junior, and senior in high school and love to teach peers who do not have any dance experience. I hope to continue choreographing in the future!

Dance has changed who I am. From my beginnings as a shy freshman lacking confidence, I have grown into a leader with an outgoing personality and the ability to communicate with others clearly and naturally. Dancing on different teams has introduced me to people from other schools and diverse backgrounds. But dance has played an even more personal role in my life.

As I found my academic and extracurricular course load increasing in high school, my stress often reached unhealthy levels. This high-demand environment left me overwhelmed and depressed, but I was able to find the route to lowering my stress through dance. As soon as I started dancing or choreographing, my stress levels fell, and I was able to calm myself and perform up to my potential. After enjoying dance as a stress reliever, I wondered if I could get others more involved in dance for the same reason.

As a result, I founded JUMP, a new club that promotes creativity and expression to relieve student stress levels through dance. After gaining school administration approval, JUMP quickly became part of my high school’s culture. We invited alumni and current students to teach their dance pieces to many different students on campus, and we participated in Lynbrook’s annual stress relief week by inviting dancers from Stanford, Columbia, Berkeley, and more prestigious universities to teach choreography. Being part of JUMP has been a stress-reducer for me as well as the majority of the club’s members as we learn from each other in a non-judgmental environment.

But performing on stage remains my favorite part of dancing. The moment before I enter the stage has always been the most exciting for me, whether I’m a performer who relishes dancing or a choreographer who loves to see other dancers grow and expand their styles. Although I suffer from stage fright immediately before a performance begins, my passion for dance makes me forget my fears and perform at my very best. Now when I see doubt and anxiety on the face of my dancers, I recall my own journey and tell them with confidence, “I did it, and I know you can, too.”

Music

Ever since I was small, music has been an important part of my life. I started learning to play the guitar at the age of 5 and I fell in love with music. I learned the basics for reading music and started to train my ears musically.

When I turned 9, I decided to pivot to alto saxophone with a focus on classical and jazz music. I first joined the school band and continued until high school, when I decided that I prefered being a soloist over a member of a band.

I continued taking private lessons for saxophone and performing as a soloist. Eventually, I decided to put my skills to the test through the Music Teachers' Association of California's music study program. With my years of experience, I decided to jump to level 6 and take the exam.

The Level 6 Exam is comprised of 3 parts: Technical Evaluation, Virtuosity Check, and Music Theory Exam. After weeks of prepration, I received my Certificate of Merit from MTAC. This certificate gave me the validation to perform in public and I started being more comfortable performing for friends and family.

After high school ended, I quit private lessons, but continue practicing guitar/alto saxophone on my own as a hobby. Although I may not practice every day, the disciplinary/creative skills that I gained made me into who I am today.

Student Government

...

I had volunteered in a variety of school organizations over my first three years, but as I neared my penultimate year of high school, I felt something was missing: a classic leadership role. So, I decided to run for the position of class Vice President. Fortunately, I was elected by my peers, and served as senior class officer.

Because I was the newest vice president on the officer team, I initially held my opinions in abeyance, believing that the other officers knew more than me. But showing deference to these experienced officers did not serve me well, as I was not taking the responsibility to represent my class assertively. I began voicing my opinions and assuming the leadership role that I aspired to fill.

One example of my newfound assertiveness came during a homecoming decoration meeting a few days before our performance. While rapidly creating posters, one of my peers was drawing on white paper to create a design with "2018 Love," but I noticed that the colors of the letters were bland, and the lines were uneven.

I approached her and said, "Hi! Thank you for spending so much time on these posters, but I noticed that there is not enough color on this one. Can I help you with the next one so we can make it even better?"

At first, she was offended by my criticism, but after working together on the next poster she agreed that the extra work was necessary: "Thanks for helping, she said. I'll make the next one even better!"

This experience taught me that a leader's responsibility is to be a positive influence by ensuring that standards are met while still showing respect for others' ideas and abilities. I realize that my judgments may not always be popular, but that it is important for me to voice them all the same. As I participated in discussions related to school and class business, I made certain to say what I think honestly and immediately. This lesson in leadership will be greatly beneficial to both myself and others for many years to come.

As class vice president, I was charged with the task of organizing senior prom, constructing a new senior activity, foster community within the class through rallies and Homecoming, and more.

DECA

WiseTech

DECA is a business-oriented organization that held competitions every year for high schoolers to pitch their ideas for companies that could solve problems around the world. For the regional and state competition in my senior year of high school, I constructed a company called WiseTech which received third place in California.

Wise technology is the next step from smart products where technology adapts to unique users, ensuring complete customer satisfaction. WiseTech is an emerging technology start-up that has innovated the first intelligent pair of socks with textile sensor technology, WiseSock, to solve an abundance of societal problems today. WiseSock can track user heart rate, immediately alert local law enforcement following a gesture, and guide the user to live a healthier lifestyle. WiseTech is an ethical company committed to improving personal health without negatively affecting the earth. Additionally, for every WiseSock sold, we donate a portion of our profits to RAINN (Rape, Abuse & Incest National Network). People around the world who purchase WiseSock will not only be healthier and safer but will also find comfort in the fact that a victim somewhere will be saved from death. Following our initial product launch, WiseTech will be continuing to develop solutions to solve worldwide problems.

Ohana Kai

Future Business Leaders of America, or FBLA, was a little more restrictive when it came to the prompts for each competition. My event was Business Financial Plan, and my team was charged to create a family entertainment center for a very specific sized-city and square footage.

Given these parameters, we created Ohana Kai which received 3rd place in California and 13th place nationally.

This unique environment offers a fun but educational environment for families to learn about the importance of marine life preservation.

Culture

...

The candle flame bursts to life as my parents help me steady the sparkler. I stare with joy as colors light up the night; this annually recurring memory is what I treasure most from my childhood. Diwali, the Indian festival of lights, has always influenced me to embrace my Indian culture.

My parents were born in India and immigrated to the United States, searching for new opportunities. Although they were far from their birthplace, they still incorporated Indian culture into my childhood. From speaking in Hindi to driving me to Hindu temples, my parents taught me to appreciate India’s festivities and history and instilled in me a strong cultural identity.

To combine my dance passion with my cultural pride, I joined my high school’s Bollywood team. Attending my dance performances around the Bay Area introduced my family and me to the active Indian community in our district.

To continue building bridges within the community, I joined the American-Indo Student Association (AISA) club as a high school sophomore. I was delighted to discover that the club gives back to the Indian community by fundraising to improve rural Indian communities. I achieved President as a senior and changed the club dynamic by expanding to be a larger part of the local community by hosting more cultural events, such as a traditional Garba Raas.

I am grateful to have had opportunities to explore my heritage and look forward to learning about other cultures throughout my life. At Berkeley, by being associated with a Bollywood team, I am well-connected to the Indian community and am able to perform at festivals, such as Diwali here.
